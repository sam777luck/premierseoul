FAQs

We provide end-to-end support for international real estate investment in Korea. From initial consultation to property registration, our team guides you through every step including legal documentation, property viewing, financing, and contract execution.

South Korea has unique rental systems: Jeonse (large deposit, no monthly rent) and Wolse (smaller deposit with monthly payments). We'll help you understand which option suits your needs and navigate the local rental market effectively.

Jeonse is a large deposit system unique to Korea, while monthly rent (Wolse) requires smaller deposits with regular payments. Jeonse typically requires a deposit of 50-80% of the property value with no monthly payments, while Wolse involves a smaller deposit plus monthly rent.

LTV (Loan-to-Value) and DSR (Debt Service Ratio) regulations vary by region and investor status. Generally, foreign investors can access 40-70% LTV depending on the property location and type. We'll guide you through the specifics for your situation.

Foreign investors are subject to acquisition tax (1-3%), property tax (annual), and capital gains tax (upon sale). We provide comprehensive tax planning to optimize your investment and ensure compliance with all regulations.

Property purchase doesn't require a specific visa, but we assist with residency-related real estate strategies. Foreign nationals can freely purchase property in Korea, though some restrictions may apply to certain areas near military zones.

We ensure proper deposit protection through legal contracts and government-backed insurance systems. Korea has tenant protection laws including deposit guarantee insurance and priority repayment rights.
